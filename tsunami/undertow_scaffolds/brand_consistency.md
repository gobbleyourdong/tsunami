## Questions
1. Is the product's logo or wordmark present in a conventional location (top-left, centred header, or footer)?
2. Is the logo rendered at a size that reads but does not dominate — not pixel-tiny, not swallowing the header?
3. Does copy voice stay consistent — no flip between formal ("We provide enterprise solutions") and casual ("hey, wanna try this?") in the same view?
4. Are capitalisation rules consistent across headings — either Title Case or sentence case, not both mixed?
5. Does every surface share one accent colour for primary actions — no "sign up" button in brand-blue next to "get started" in a different hue?
6. Is iconography from one family — all outline, all filled, or all duotone, not mixed?
7. Are buttons and form controls visually identical across the view — same radius, same padding, same weight?
8. Does the product name appear spelled and cased identically every time it is written?
9. Is the view free of competing brand assets — no stray placeholder logo, template watermark, or "made with X" badge?
10. Do imagery and illustrations share a style — no lone stock photo inside an otherwise flat-illustration system?

## PASS criteria
≥ 8/10 questions answer yes unambiguously.

## FAIL criteria
Any "no" on questions 1, 5, or 8 (missing logo, inconsistent primary-action colour, or misspelled / miscased product name — these break brand trust on sight).
