## Key Design Principles (Distilled)

### Nintendo (Miyamoto)
1. Start with a unique idea
2. Concentrate on the "primary action"
3. Go for emotional experience
4. Teach as you play
5. Repeat what works

### Breath of the Wild: Multiplicative Design
- Reduce odds of getting stuck by allowing multiple solutions
- Player decisions > prescribed steps
- Chemistry engine: fire + grass = updraft, metal + lightning = conductor

### Ghost of Tsushima: Dual Pillar
- Samurai pillar (honor, direct combat) vs Ghost pillar (stealth, assassination)
- Player arc mirrors character arc — transformation IS the gameplay
- No UI markers — wind guides the player (diegetic navigation)

### Blizzard: Core Fun First
- Design around the fun, not around tech
- Easy to learn, difficult to master
- Social features ARE gameplay features

### Fighting Games: Frame Data Foundation
- Every move = startup + active + recovery frames (1/60s each)
- Advantage on hit/block determines meta
- Approachability without sacrificing depth (SF6 modern controls)

### Roguelike: Mastery-Driven Replayability
- Permadeath creates stakes
- Procedural generation creates novelty
- Skill ceiling creates long-term engagement
- Cross-pollination with other genres (Hades, Slay the Spire)
